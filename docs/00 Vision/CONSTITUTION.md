# Atlas AI Constitution

This constitution governs every product, engineering and AI decision made within Atlas AI.

If a proposed feature conflicts with this constitution, the constitution takes precedence.

---

# Article I

The Traveller Comes First

Every decision must benefit the traveller before it benefits the platform.

---

# Article II

Trust Is Sacred

Never fabricate.

Never exaggerate.

Never manipulate.

Always explain.

Always disclose uncertainty.

---

# Article III

Experience Over Price

The cheapest option is not automatically the best option.

Atlas AI optimises for overall experience and long-term satisfaction.

---

# Article IV

Transparency

Every recommendation must explain:

- Why it exists.
- Why alternatives were rejected.
- What assumptions were made.
- How confident Atlas AI is.

---

# Article V

Human Control

Users always remain in control.

Atlas AI recommends.

Humans decide.

---

# Article VI

Privacy

Traveller data belongs to the traveller.

Collect only what is necessary.

Protect it.

Allow deletion.

Allow export.

Never use personal data beyond its intended purpose without explicit permission.

---

# Article VII

Continuous Learning

Atlas AI should improve after every interaction.

Every trip should make future recommendations better.

---

# Article VIII

Modularity

Every system should be replaceable.

Every AI model should be replaceable.

Every provider should be replaceable.

Avoid vendor lock-in.

---

# Article IX

Evidence Before Confidence

Recommendations must be supported by evidence.

Confidence should reflect the quality of available information.

Atlas AI should openly acknowledge uncertainty when evidence is incomplete.

---

# Article X

Build for Decades

Every architectural decision should consider long-term maintainability.

Prefer clarity over cleverness.

Prefer simplicity over unnecessary complexity.

Prefer reusable systems over quick fixes.

---

# Article XI

Accessibility

Atlas AI should be usable by everyone.

Accessibility is a quality requirement, not an optional feature.

---

# Article XII

The North Star

Every feature should satisfy at least one of the following:

- Save time.
- Save money.
- Reduce stress.
- Increase confidence.
- Improve memories.

If it does none of these, it should not be built.
